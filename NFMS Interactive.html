When to Use the NFMS

The NFMS is NST's primary outcome measure. Administer at intake and at regular intervals. Always check capacity first — if below 3/10, consider postponing.

Appropriate populations

Autistic adults (diagnosed or self-identified) · Adults with ADHD · Multiple neurodevelopmental differences · Late-diagnosed individuals · Anyone experiencing autistic burnout or chronic overwhelm secondary to environmental mismatch

Scope decision guide

🟢
Proceed with NST

Clear person–environment mismatch · Capacity chronically depleted despite adequate sleep/basic self-care · Symptoms secondary to overload · Client has some control over environment · No active safety crisis · Client open to or neutral about the mismatch frame

🟡
Proceed with consultation

Complex trauma alongside mismatch · PDA profile · Multiple diagnoses with unclear primary driver · Progress stalled and cause unclear · Client resistant to neurodiversity framing · Systems changes would require major life restructuring

🔴
Refer or coordinate first

Active SI with plan and intent · Psychosis · Eating disorder with medical instability · Substance use requiring detox · Active mania · IPV with safety concerns · Acute housing instability. NST can be adjunctive once crisis is stabilised — pause interventions, not the relationship.

Administration Protocol

  1. Check capacity. Ask client for a capacity rating (0–10). Below 3, postpone or shorten. Capacity variability is expected — never interpret it as resistance.
  2. Provide client instructions — read aloud or in writing. Key phrase: "based on your typical experience over the past two weeks."
  3. Allow 10–20 minutes for completion. Client may complete independently or with support. No right or wrong answers.
  4. Submit and score. The tool handles all reverse-scoring and subscale calculation automatically.
  5. Review collaboratively. Use the prompts in the results view. Client experience takes precedence over numeric interpretation.
  6. Apply the Priority Formula. Rate environmental modifiability (ε) for each domain. Severity alone does not determine priority.
  7. Document and target 1–2 subscales scoring ≤ 3.0 as initial intervention targets.
Timing: Baseline (Session 1) · Every 4–6 sessions · Mid-treatment (~Sessions 10–12) · Post-treatment · 3-month follow-up

Score Interpretation — Quick Reference

Subscale means (1.0–5.0)

1.0–2.0 Severe Mismatch — Immediate, intensive intervention. May indicate crisis.

2.1–3.0 Significant Mismatch — Primary treatment target. Substantial environmental changes needed.

3.1–3.5 Moderate Mismatch — Active treatment target. Secondary priority after severe/significant.

3.6–4.0 Mild Mismatch — Fine-tuning. Later phases or maintenance.

4.1–5.0 Good Fit — Preserve and protect. Leverage as resource for other areas.

Total score (39–195)

39–78 Severe · Phase 1 Survival  |  79–117 Significant · Phase 2 Stabilisation  |  118–137 Moderate · Phase 3  |  138–156 Mild  |  157–195 Good Fit · Phase 4 Thriving

Priority Formula

Priority(i) = (5.0 − subscale mean) × ε (environmental modifiability, 0–1). Calculated interactively in the results view.

Stall rule

No ≥0.5 improvement after 4–6 focused sessions → audit: capacity (overestimated?), mismatch domain (correct?), narrative injury (blocking implementation?), scope (needs resources beyond therapy?).

Core NST Concepts

MOD Cycle

Mismatch → Overload → Distress → Narrative Injury (amplifies effective demand) → more Mismatch. Self-amplifying. Both environment and narrative must be addressed.

Dual Movement

Track 1 — Systems Alignment: Modify the environment. Track 2 — Narrative Repair: Address harmful self-narratives. Both tracks must be active. Ratio by phase: Phase 1 (80/20 Systems/Narrative) · Phase 2 (50/50) · Phase 3 (40/60) · Phase 4 (20/80).

Capacity

Dynamic — fluctuates with sleep, nutrition, sensory load, emotion, recovery. Check every session (0–10). Low capacity and lack of progress are clinical data, not resistance.

Narrative Injury as Phantom Demand

Harmful self-narratives ("I'm broken," "I should cope") add real physiological demand. High Narrative Injury scores explain why environmental changes alone often haven't helped.

The form includes a built-in accessibility toolbar (font size, high contrast, reduce motion, dyslexia mode, larger buttons).
